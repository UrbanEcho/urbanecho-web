Nairobi's mobility landscape is shaped by rapid urban growth, an extensive informal matatu network, and significant disparities in accessibility across neighbourhoods. These dynamics make the city a high-impact candidate for an upcoming UrbanEcho digital twin — one focused on understanding mobility behaviour, accessibility gaps, and the potential of formalised transport improvements.

As we prepare Nairobi's digital twin, UrbanEcho will offer tools to explore how future mobility solutions, network restructuring, and land-use changes can shape equitable access across the metropolitan region.

### Why collaborate now

We're at the stage where the first scenarios need to be defined — which corridors to model, which equity questions to ask, which integration points between formal and informal networks matter most. That requires local knowledge. UrbanEcho is looking for planners, consultants, NGOs, and researchers who want to help shape what this twin becomes before the models are locked in

UrbanEcho is seeking early partners — planners, consultants, NGOs, and researchers — to help shape Nairobi's first scenarios and define priority layers for the digital twin.
