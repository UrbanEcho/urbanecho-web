Dar es Salaam's mobility landscape is shaped by rapid urban growth, an expanding DART BRT network, widespread informal and paratransit modes, and a surge in e-mobility pilots from e-bajajs to e-bikes. These dynamics make the city a high-impact candidate for an upcoming UrbanEcho digital twin — one focused on understanding how formal and informal networks interact, where charging and electrification can scale, and how equitable access evolves across the metropolitan region.

As we prepare Dar es Salaam's digital twin, UrbanEcho will offer tools to explore how future mobility solutions, BRT integration, and electrification pathways can shape equitable access across the city and its corridors.

### Why collaborate now

We're at the stage where the first scenarios need to be defined — which corridors to model, which equity questions to ask, which integration points between DART, informal feeders, and e-mobility pilots matter most. That requires local knowledge. UrbanEcho is looking for planners, consultants, NGOs, and researchers who want to help shape what this twin becomes before the models are locked in.

UrbanEcho is seeking early partners — planners, utilities, e-mobility operators, NGOs, and researchers — to help shape Dar es Salaam's first scenarios and define priority layers for the digital twin.
